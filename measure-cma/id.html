How to identify Usnea

Generally my process for identifying Usnea in my area (the Pacific Northwest) is to fill out the following information:

Growth form Pendulous / tufted
Branch shape Cylindrical / cigar-shaped
Other branch features None / branch ends sinuose / branches with ridges and valleys
Annular cracks Many / some / none
Papillae Many / At base only / none
Fibrils Abundant / some / few or none
Reproductive structures Isidia only / isidia in young/all soralia / soralia lacking isidia / none or apotheciate
Soralia morphology (if applicable) Soralia small / soralia large, >1/2 branch diameter / cortex recurving around soralia / soralia deep with axis visible
CMA 10 / 20 / 40
UV + / -
Spot tests (if applicable) P+O

Observing

There is a large amount of variance withing Usnea, and it can be easy to overlook telltale signs of species. I find the above information is best done under a dissecting microscope at around 40x magnification. It's best to take a long time here and not to draw conclusions too quickly; for example, rather than noting the first soralia you see, it's better to look at many and note what a normal soralia appears, as well as note differences between young and old soralia.

Spot tests

As many sources note, spot tests are unreliable and usually only "corroborate" an existing identification. UV can be tricky as well, as some species are only weakly UV+. I take strong, clear reactions as being conclusive and negative reactions as being unconclusive.

How to make CMA measurements

Sources generally recommend making cross-sections on thick branches, with Philippe Clerc taking them on the "thickest part of the thickest branch" or McCune and Geiser recommending using a "main but not basal" branch.

It can sometimes be useful to gently wet the thallus if it's dry, but usually I can make cuts equally easily on wet and dessicated thalli.

I identify a good relatively exposed spot under a stereo microscope at ~15x magnification. I then rotate the thallus so it's at a comfortable angle to cut (for me this is usually at a 45° angle, with my hand securing the thallus to the bottom left). I then hold down the thallus with my non-dominant hand and focus using the scope, then gently move away other fibrils using forceps. I make the cut directly on the thallus, gently sawing with my right hand. It's important that the cut runs directly down the middle of the branch. If I notice that I haven't cut deeply enough (the axis not being visible or being obscured in front by the medulla is a sign of this), I'll make another cut until it's more or less correct.

After making the cut, I shine my UV lamp on it and note if it fluoresces or not, and how strongly. I then align the thallus so it's horizontal, and zoom in as closely as possible to take a photo through the microscope oculars using my phone. I crop this image on my phone so the branch is in the lower 2/3rds of the frame with some space above for the label, then upload to the CMA measurement tool and make the measurement there. I usually fill in the label field just with my herbarium number and download the image at this point.
